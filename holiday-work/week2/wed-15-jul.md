## Session 1 — English · 45 min · Language Paper 1, Q4 Evaluation

**Today's focus: Q4 — Evaluation (20 marks)**

Q4 gives you a student's opinion about the text and asks how far you agree. Structure: **agree with evidence → nuance/partial disagreement → overall judgement.** Do not just agree, and do not just disagree — the top marks go to a balanced argument that reaches a clear personal judgement.

### Extract — Read carefully (5 min)

> *Marcus had worked at the lighthouse for thirty-one years, and in all that time no one had ever stayed the night. Visitors came in summer, snapping photographs of the white tower against the cliffs, asking him the same three questions — was it lonely, had he seen a shipwreck, did the light ever go out — and then they left on the four o'clock ferry, glad, he suspected, to be leaving.*
>
> *He didn't mind the questions. What he minded was the assumption behind them: that a man who chose solitude must be running from something, or missing something, or broken in some quiet way that explained his choice. He had tried, once or twice, to explain that the lighthouse gave him what other people seemed to look for their whole lives and never find — a job that mattered, done well, with no one watching. But the explanation always sounded thin against their sympathy, so mostly he just said yes, it gets quiet, and let them believe what they needed to believe.*
>
> *Tonight the wind was picking up, and he climbed the spiral stairs slowly, the way he always did, counting them without meaning to. One hundred and forty-two steps. He knew every one of them by the sound it made underfoot. At the top, the lamp room smelled of oil and salt, and through the glass he could see the shape of a container ship far out, its lights blinking red and white in a rhythm he'd learned to read like a second language.*
>
> *He logged the time. He checked the lamp. He sat, as he did every night, in the chair by the window, and watched the dark water do what it had done for longer than the lighthouse itself had stood there. It did not feel like loneliness. It felt like being trusted with something.*

### Task — Evaluation, timed (20 min)

> A student, having read this section of text, said: "The writer makes Marcus seem like a genuinely lonely, isolated figure." To what extent do you agree?
>
> Write about: your own impressions of Marcus · how the writer has created these impressions · [20 marks]

**Plan first (5 min) — jot down:**
- 2–3 points that **support** the statement (evidence of isolation)
- 1–2 points that **complicate or challenge** it (evidence he is content, not lonely)
- Your **overall judgement** — is "lonely" the right word, or is there a better one?

**Write your response below (20 min):**

*(Aim for 4–5 paragraphs: agree, agree, nuance, nuance, judgement.)*
