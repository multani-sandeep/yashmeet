## Session 1 — Maths · 45 min · Quadratics Warm-up + Trigonometry (SOH CAH TOA)

### Part A — Quadratics Warm-up (10 min)

Solve using any method:
1. x² − 5x + 6 = 0
2. x² − 9 = 0
3. x² + 2x − 8 = 0

**Answers:** x = 2 or 3 · x = 3 or −3 · x = 2 or −4

### Part B — SOH CAH TOA (20 min)

For a right-angled triangle:
- **S**in θ = **O**pposite / **H**ypotenuse
- **C**os θ = **A**djacent / **H**ypotenuse
- **T**an θ = **O**pposite / **A**djacent

Find the missing side or angle in each right-angled triangle (round to 1 d.p.):

1. Hypotenuse = 12 cm, angle = 35°. Find the opposite side.
2. Adjacent = 8 cm, angle = 50°. Find the hypotenuse.
3. Opposite = 6 cm, adjacent = 9 cm. Find angle θ.
4. Hypotenuse = 15 cm, opposite = 7 cm. Find angle θ.
5. Adjacent = 10 cm, angle = 42°. Find the opposite side.
6. Opposite = 4.5 cm, hypotenuse = 9 cm. Find angle θ.

**Answers:** 6.9 cm · 12.4 cm · 33.7° · 27.8° · 9.0 cm · 30.0°

## Session 2 — Chemistry · 45 min · Bonding Recap

### Part A — Quick-fire Matching (15 min)

Match each substance/property description to a bonding type (**Ionic / Covalent-simple / Covalent-giant / Metallic**):

1. NaCl — high melting point, conducts only when molten or dissolved
2. H₂O — low melting point, does not conduct electricity
3. Diamond — extremely hard, very high melting point, does not conduct
4. Copper — malleable, conducts electricity in solid form
5. CO₂ — low melting point, exists as small molecules
6. Graphite — high melting point, conducts electricity, layers slide over each other

### Part B — Explain the Conductivity (15 min)

For each, explain **why** in terms of particles and structure (2–3 sentences each):
1. Why does NaCl conduct electricity when molten but not when solid?
2. Why does copper conduct electricity in the solid state?
3. Why does simple covalent H₂O never conduct electricity?
4. Why does graphite conduct electricity but diamond does not, even though both are pure carbon?

### Part C — Exam Question (15 min)

> Diamond and graphite are both giant covalent structures made entirely of carbon atoms.
>
> (a) Describe the structure of diamond, including the number of bonds per carbon atom. [2]
> (b) Describe the structure of graphite, including the number of bonds per carbon atom. [2]
> (c) Explain why graphite can conduct electricity and is used as a lubricant, while diamond cannot conduct and is extremely hard. [4]

## Secondary — History · 30 min · Source Utility (Western Front)

**Skill: Source Utility — content, provenance, own knowledge**

### Sources

**Source A** — Extract from a letter written by a British soldier to his family, October 1916, during the Battle of the Somme:
> "We have been in the line eight days now and the mud is beyond anything I could have described to you before I came out here. Two of the lads in my section have gone down with trench foot already. We keep our spirits up as best we can, though the guns never seem to stop for long."

**Source B** — Photograph caption from a British government-approved newspaper, 1916:
> "Our brave boys, cheerful as ever, share a joke in a well-drained forward trench near the front line. Morale remains high as the advance continues."

### Task (20 min)
