SESSION 2 - CHEMISTRY (45 min) - Moles
---------------------------------------------------------

Part A - Mr and moles:
1. Mr of NaOH        = 40
2. Mr of CaCO3        = 100
3. Mr of Mg(OH)2      = 58
4. Mr of (NH4)2SO4    = 132

5. Moles in 20g NaOH (working):
n = 20/40
    Answer = 0.5 mol

6. Mass of 0.5 mol CaCO3 (working):
m = 0.5 x 100
    Answer = 50 g

7. Moles in 4.4g CO2 (working):
n = 4.4/44
    Answer = 0.1 mol

Part B - Reacting masses (Mg + 2HCl -> MgCl2 + H2):

1. Moles of Mg in 6g (working):
n = 6/24
    Answer = 0.25 mol

2. Moles of H2 produced (working):
1:1 ratio with Mg
    Answer = 0.25 mol

3. Mass of MgCl2 produced (working):
n(MgCl2) = 0.25 mol (1:1 with Mg)
m = 0.25 x 95
    Answer = 23.75 g

Part C - Empirical formula (C: 2.4g, H: 0.4g, O: 3.2g):

(a) Moles of each element (working):
C: 2.4/12 = 0.2 mol
H: 0.4/1  = 0.4 mol
O: 3.2/16 = 0.2 mol

(b) Simplest ratio (divide by smallest, 0.2):
C : H : O = 1 : 2 : 1

(c) Empirical formula:
    Answer = CH2O
