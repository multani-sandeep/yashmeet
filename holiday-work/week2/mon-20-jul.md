## Session 2 — Chemistry · 45 min · Moles and Quantitative Chemistry

### Part A — Mole Formula Triangle (15 min)

n = m / Mr (moles = mass ÷ relative formula mass)

Calculate the relative formula mass (Mr) of:
1. NaOH
2. CaCO₃
3. Mg(OH)₂
4. (NH₄)₂SO₄

Then calculate:
5. Moles in 20 g of NaOH
6. Mass of 0.5 mol of CaCO₃
7. Moles in 4.4 g of CO₂

### Part B — Reacting Masses (15 min)

> Magnesium reacts with hydrochloric acid: Mg + 2HCl → MgCl₂ + H₂
>
> 1. Calculate the number of moles in 6 g of magnesium (Ar of Mg = 24).
> 2. Using the equation, calculate the moles of H₂ produced.
> 3. Calculate the mass of MgCl₂ produced (Mr of MgCl₂ = 95).

### Part C — Exam Question: Empirical Formula (15 min)

> A compound contains 2.4 g of carbon, 0.4 g of hydrogen, and 3.2 g of oxygen.
>
> (a) Calculate the number of moles of each element. (Ar: C = 12, H = 1, O = 16) [2]
> (b) Divide by the smallest number of moles to find the simplest ratio. [2]
> (c) State the empirical formula of the compound. [1]

## Secondary — History · 30 min · 12-Mark Extended Writing (PEEL)

**Skill: 12-mark essay — three developed paragraphs, PEEL structure**

> Explain why medical care improved on the Western Front during the First World War. You may use the following in your answer and your own knowledge: **X-rays** · **blood transfusion** [12 marks]

**PEEL structure for each paragraph:**
> Point: One reason why medical care improved was [cause].
> Evidence: [specific fact, date, name].
> Explanation: This led to improved medical care because [mechanism].
> Link: This was significant because...

### Plan and write (25 min)

Write **three** PEEL paragraphs — one on X-rays, one on blood transfusion, and one more reason from your own knowledge (e.g. the RAMC's stretcher-bearer system, or advances in treating shell shock).
