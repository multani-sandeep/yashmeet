## Session 1 — English · 45 min · Language Paper 1, Q5 Descriptive Writing

**Today's focus: Q5 — Descriptive writing (40 marks), timed**

Descriptive writing is marked on: vocabulary and sentence variety, structure and control of pace, and creating atmosphere ("show, don't tell"). Avoid clichés. Spend as much effort on your ending as your opening.

### Stimulus (2 min)

> Write a description suggested by this title: **"The Night Market"**

### Plan (10 min)

Before writing, jot down:
- One dominant **mood/atmosphere** you want to create (e.g. overwhelming, magical, chaotic, alive)
- 3–4 specific **sensory details** (not generic — think of one unusual smell, one specific sound, one precise visual detail)
- A structural idea: how will you move through the scene? (e.g. wide shot → zoom into one stallholder → wide shot again; or a journey from the edge of the market to its centre)
- Your opening sentence and your closing sentence — plan both before you write the middle

### Write your response (30 min, timed)

*(Aim for 350–450 words. Vary sentence length. Use precise nouns and verbs rather than piling up adjectives.)*

### Self-check (5 min)

| Check | ✓ / ✗ |
|---|---|
| Did I use specific, unusual sensory detail rather than generic description? | |
| Did I vary sentence length for effect (short for impact, long for atmosphere)? | |
| Does my structure move somewhere (not just a static list of things seen)? | |
| Is my ending as carefully crafted as my opening? | |

## Session 2 — Biology · 45 min · Enzymes Recap

### Part A — Lock and Key Model (10 min)

1. Define **enzyme** in terms of its role as a biological catalyst.
2. Describe the lock and key model: what is the "lock" and what is the "key"?
3. What is meant by an enzyme's **active site**, and what happens if the substrate does not fit it?

### Part B — Temperature and pH Effects (15 min)

1. Sketch (describe in words) the shape of a graph of **rate of reaction vs temperature** for an enzyme. Label the optimum.
2. Explain what happens to enzyme activity **below** the optimum temperature (in terms of particle movement/kinetic energy).
3. Explain what happens to enzyme activity **above** the optimum temperature. Use the term **denature** and explain what happens to the active site's shape.
4. Explain why enzymes in the stomach (pepsin) work best at a low pH, while enzymes in the small intestine work best at a higher pH.

### Part C — Exam Question (20 min)

> A student investigates the effect of temperature on the rate of reaction of the enzyme catalase, which breaks down hydrogen peroxide into water and oxygen. They measure the volume of oxygen gas produced in 60 seconds at temperatures of 10°C, 20°C, 30°C, 40°C, and 50°C.
>
> (a) Describe the independent, dependent, and one control variable in this experiment. [3]
> (b) Predict and explain the results you would expect at 40°C compared with 30°C, if 37°C is the optimum temperature for this enzyme. [3]
> (c) Predict and explain the results you would expect at 50°C. [3]
> (d) Suggest one improvement to the method to make the results more reliable. [1]

## Secondary — Computing · 30 min · Algorithms

### Part A — Flowchart Symbols (10 min)

Name the flowchart symbol for each purpose:
- Start/stop = ___
- A process or action = ___
- A yes/no decision = ___
- Input or output = ___

Trace this flowchart by hand and state the final output:
```
START
count ← 0
total ← 0
WHILE count < 5
    total ← total + count
    count ← count + 1
ENDWHILE
OUTPUT total
STOP
```

### Part B — Linear vs Binary Search (10 min)

Given the sorted list: `[3, 7, 12, 18, 24, 31, 40, 52]`

1. Trace a **linear search** for the value 31. How many comparisons does it take?
2. Trace a **binary search** for the value 31 (show the middle value checked at each step). How many comparisons does it take?
3. State one condition binary search requires that linear search does not.

### Part C — Exam Question (10 min)

> A list of 1,000 sorted student ID numbers is searched to find a specific ID.
>
> (a) Explain why binary search is more efficient than linear search for this list. [2]
> (b) State what would need to happen to the list before binary search could be used, if it were not already sorted. [1]
> (c) Give one real-world situation where linear search would be the only option, even though it's slower. [1]
