## Session 2 — Physics · 45 min · Forces: Moments and Pressure (Y10 Preview)

### Part A — Moments (15 min)

Moment = force × perpendicular distance from pivot (unit: Nm). **Principle of moments:** sum of clockwise moments = sum of anticlockwise moments, for an object in equilibrium.

1. A force of 20 N is applied 0.5 m from a pivot. Calculate the moment.
2. A seesaw is balanced. A 400 N weight sits 1.2 m from the pivot on the left. Calculate the distance from the pivot a 300 N weight must sit on the right to balance it.
3. A spanner applies a force of 15 N at a perpendicular distance of 0.2 m from a nut. Calculate the moment applied to the nut.

### Part B — Pressure (15 min)

P = F/A (pressure = force ÷ area, unit: Pa or N/m²) · P = ρgh (pressure in a liquid = density × gravitational field strength × height)

1. A box weighing 240 N rests on a surface of area 0.4 m². Calculate the pressure it exerts.
2. Calculate the pressure at a depth of 5 m in water (density = 1000 kg/m³, g = 10 N/kg).
3. Explain why a drawing pin has a sharp point but a wide, flat head.

### Part C — Hooke's Law + Exam Question (15 min)

Hooke's Law: F = ke (extension is proportional to force, up to the elastic limit)

1. A spring has spring constant 40 N/m. Calculate the force needed to extend it by 0.15 m.

> Exam question: A student stretches a spring with increasing force and measures the extension.
>
> (a) Sketch (describe in words) the shape of a force-extension graph up to and beyond the elastic limit. [2]
> (b) The spring extends 6 cm under a force of 12 N, within its elastic limit. Calculate the spring constant in N/m. [2]
> (c) Explain, in terms of forces, why the spring returns to its original length when the force is removed (below the elastic limit) but not beyond it. [2]
