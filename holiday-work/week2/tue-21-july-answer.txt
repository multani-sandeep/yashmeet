Part B - Pressure:

1. Pressure (working):

    Answer = 600 Pa

2. Pressure at 5m depth (working):
P = 1000 x 10 x 5
    Answer = 50,000 Pa (50 kPa)

3. Why drawing pin has sharp point + flat head:
Sharp point = small area = high pressure for a given force (pierces material).
Flat head = large area = low pressure (comfortable to push without hurting finger).

Part C - Hooke's Law:

1. Force needed (working):
F = 40 x 0.15
    Answer = 6 N

Exam question:

(a) Shape of force-extension graph:
Straight line through the origin up to the elastic limit, then curves
(no longer proportional) beyond the elastic limit.

(b) Spring constant (working):
k = F / e = 12 / 0.06
    Answer = 200 N/m

(c) Why it returns to original length (below elastic limit) but not
beyond it:
Below the elastic limit, the deformation is elastic - the material's
structure is not permanently changed, so it returns to its original
shape. Beyond the elastic limit, the deformation becomes plastic -
the structure is permanently altered and does not fully recover.
