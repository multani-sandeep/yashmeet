## Y9 Grammar Summary — Tenses

GCSE Spanish requires confident use of **at least three tenses** in writing and speaking. These are the three core tenses from Y9:

### Present Tense (El Presente)
Regular patterns: -AR → -o/-as/-a/-amos/-áis/-an · -ER → -o/-es/-e/-emos/-éis/-en · -IR → same as -ER with minor differences

Key irregular verbs to know cold:

| Verb | Yo | Tú | Él/Ella | Nosotros |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ser (to be — permanent) | soy | eres | es | somos |
| estar (to be — temporary) | estoy | estás | está | estamos |
| tener (to have) | tengo | tienes | tiene | tenemos |
| ir (to go) | voy | vas | va | vamos |
| hacer (to do/make) | hago | haces | hace | hacemos |
| querer (to want) | quiero | quieres | quiere | queremos |
| poder (to be able) | puedo | puedes | puede | podemos |
| venir (to come) | vengo | vienes | viene | venimos |

---

### Preterite Tense — Past (El Pretérito Indefinido)
Use for: completed, one-off actions in the past ("I ate", "she went", "we visited")

**Regular -AR verbs** (hablar → to speak):
hablé · hablaste · habló · hablamos · hablasteis · hablaron

**Regular -ER/-IR verbs** (comer → to eat / vivir → to live):
comí · comiste · comió · comimos · comisteis · comieron

**Key irregular preterites** (must memorise — no pattern):

| Verb | Yo | Tú | Él/Ella | Nosotros |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ser / ir (same forms!) | fui | fuiste | fue | fuimos |
| tener | tuve | tuviste | tuvo | tuvimos |
| hacer | hice | hiciste | hizo | hicimos |
| estar | estuve | estuviste | estuvo | estuvimos |
| poder | pude | pudiste | pudo | pudimos |
| venir | vine | viniste | vino | vinimos |
| decir | dije | dijiste | dijo | dijimos |

---

### Near Future Tense (El Futuro Próximo)
Use for: plans and intentions ("I am going to…")

**Formula:** IR (present tense) + A + infinitive

- Voy a comer → I am going to eat
- Vas a salir → You are going to go out
- Va a hacer → He/She is going to do
- Vamos a visitar → We are going to visit

---

### Simple Future Tense (El Futuro Simple)
Use for: predictions and future events ("I will…")

**Regular formation:** add endings to the infinitive:
-é · -ás · -á · -emos · -éis · -án

- hablaré (I will speak) · comerás (you will eat) · vivirá (he will live)

**Irregular stems** (endings are the same, stem changes):

| Verb | Irregular stem |
|---|---|
| tener | tendr- |
| hacer | har- |
| poder | podr- |
| venir | vendr- |
| ir | ir- |
| ser | ser- |
| saber | sabr- |
| querer | querr- |

Example: tendré (I will have) · harás (you will do) · podrá (he will be able)

---

### Imperfect Tense (El Imperfecto)
Use for: ongoing or habitual past actions ("I used to…", "I was …-ing", "It was…")

**Regular -AR verbs:** hablaba · hablabas · hablaba · hablábamos · hablabais · hablaban

**Regular -ER/-IR verbs:** comía · comías · comía · comíamos · comíais · comían

**Only 3 irregular imperfect verbs:**
- ser: era · eras · era · éramos · erais · eran
- ir: iba · ibas · iba · íbamos · ibais · iban
- ver: veía · veías · veía · veíamos · veíais · veían

**Preterite vs Imperfect — the key distinction:**
- Preterite: "Yesterday I **went** to the market." (completed event)
- Imperfect: "Every Saturday I **used to go** to the market." (habitual/routine)
- Imperfect: "It **was** sunny and the birds **were** singing." (background description)

---

### Reflexive Verbs
Reflexive verbs need a reflexive pronoun before the conjugated verb:
me · te · se · nos · os · se

**Daily routine verbs (high-frequency):**

| Infinitive | Meaning | Yo form |
|---|---|---|
| levantarse | to get up | me levanto |
| despertarse (e→ie) | to wake up | me despierto |
| ducharse | to shower | me ducho |
| lavarse | to wash | me lavo |
| vestirse (e→i) | to get dressed | me visto |
| acostarse (o→ue) | to go to bed | me acuesto |
| llamarse | to be called | me llamo |

## Key Vocabulary Topics (GCSE Foundation — Viva 3 Rojo)

### Environment and Global Issues
- el medio ambiente (environment) · el cambio climático (climate change) · la contaminación (pollution)
- los recursos naturales (natural resources) · la deforestación (deforestation) · el reciclaje (recycling)
- *Se debe* + infinitive = one must / people should: *Se debe reciclar más* (People should recycle more)
- *Hay que* + infinitive = it is necessary to: *Hay que proteger el planeta*

### Health and Lifestyle
- *Me duele* + body part = my [body part] hurts: *Me duele la cabeza* (My head hurts)
- *Me duelen* + plural = my [things] hurt: *Me duelen los pies*
- hacer ejercicio (to exercise) · llevar una dieta sana (to have a healthy diet) · el estrés (stress)

### Work and Future Plans
- *Quisiera ser* (I would like to be) · *Espero* + infinitive (I hope to) · *Mi sueño es* (My dream is)
- trabajar (to work) · ganar dinero (to earn money) · la carrera (career)

### Using Three Tenses Together
In any extended writing task, aim to include:
1. A sentence in the **present**: "Normalmente como…" (Normally I eat…)
2. A sentence in the **preterite**: "Ayer fui al…" (Yesterday I went to…)
3. A sentence in the **future**: "El año que viene voy a…" (Next year I am going to…)
4. A sentence in the **imperfect** (bonus): "Cuando era pequeño…" (When I was young…)

## Holiday Opportunity — Spanish Immersion

The trip to Australia and India includes airports, hotels, restaurants, and tourist sites. Make a habit of:
- Mentally forming sentences in Spanish about what you observe ("Esta playa es más bonita que…")
- Keeping a brief Spanish diary (2–3 sentences per day) about the trip — this directly practises three-tense writing in a meaningful context
- Using Duolingo or a similar app for 5–10 minutes during travel downtime (good for vocabulary maintenance)
