## Y9 Recap (Quick Review — 1 session each)

These topics were examined in Y9. Do a quick pass on any that felt uncertain.

### Electricity (Textbook §2.1–2.8)
Key formulae:
- V = IR (Ohm's Law: voltage = current × resistance)
- P = IV = I²R = V²/R (power)
- E = Pt (energy = power × time)
- Q = It (charge = current × time)

Series circuits: same current throughout; voltages add up; resistances add up  
Parallel circuits: voltage same across each branch; currents add up; total resistance is less than smallest branch

Past paper questions: p.86 (Q1–19) in textbook

### Waves, Light, and Sound (Textbook §3.1–3.8)
Key formulae:
- v = fλ (wave speed = frequency × wavelength)
- Snell's Law: n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂

Reflection: angle of incidence = angle of reflection (measured from the normal)  
Refraction: light bends toward normal when entering a denser medium  
Total Internal Reflection: occurs when angle of incidence exceeds the critical angle in a denser medium

Past paper questions: p.121 (Q1–14, 16–19) in textbook

### Mechanics — Energy and Motion (Textbook §4.1–4.5)
Key formulae:
- KE = ½mv² (kinetic energy)
- GPE = mgh (gravitational potential energy)
- W = Fd (work done = force × distance)
- P = W/t (power = work done / time)

Distance-time graphs: gradient = speed  
Velocity-time graphs: gradient = acceleration; area under graph = distance

Past paper questions: p.154 (Q1, 5–6, 8–10, 12–15, 19–20) in textbook

### Forces (Y10 Unit)
Key concepts:
- **Resultant force:** vector sum of all forces on an object
- **Newton's Laws:**
  - 1st: An object stays at rest or constant velocity unless a resultant force acts
  - 2nd: F = ma (force = mass × acceleration)
  - 3rd: Every action has an equal and opposite reaction
- **Moments:** moment = force × perpendicular distance from pivot (unit: Nm)
- **Principle of moments:** sum of clockwise moments = sum of anticlockwise moments (for equilibrium)
- **Pressure:** P = F/A (pressure = force / area, unit: Pa or N/m²)
- **Pressure in liquids:** P = ρgh (density × g × height)
- **Hooke's Law:** F = ke (extension is proportional to force, up to elastic limit)

Practice: draw free body diagrams for an object in equilibrium; calculate moments; apply Newton's 2nd Law.

---

### Thermal Physics (Y10 Unit)
Key concepts:
- **Specific heat capacity:** E = mcΔT (energy = mass × specific heat capacity × temperature change)
- **Latent heat:** energy needed to change state without changing temperature; E = mL
- **Kinetic theory of gases:**
  - Molecules move randomly and collide with walls → this is pressure
  - Increase temperature → molecules move faster → more/harder collisions → higher pressure
- **Ideal Gas Law:** P₁V₁/T₁ = P₂V₂/T₂ (pressure × volume / temperature = constant)
  - Temperature must always be in **Kelvin** (K = °C + 273)

Worked example: A gas has pressure 100 kPa, volume 2 m³, at 300 K. Find new pressure if volume halves at same temperature.
P₁V₁/T₁ = P₂V₂/T₂ → 100 × 2 / 300 = P₂ × 1 / 300 → P₂ = 200 kPa

---

### Magnetism and Electromagnetism (Y10 Unit)
Key concepts:
- Permanent magnets: north and south poles; field lines go from N to S outside the magnet
- **Electromagnetism:** a current-carrying conductor creates a magnetic field around it; direction given by right-hand grip rule
- **Solenoid:** coil of wire — behaves like a bar magnet when current flows; acts as an electromagnet
- **Force on a current in a magnetic field:** F = BIL (flux density × current × length); direction given by Fleming's left-hand rule
  - Thumb = Force (motion), Index = Field, Middle = Current
- **Electromagnetic induction:** moving a conductor through a field (or changing field through a coil) induces a voltage — Fleming's right-hand rule
- **Transformers:** step-up (more turns secondary → higher voltage); step-down (fewer turns → lower voltage)
  - V₁/V₂ = N₁/N₂ (voltage ratio = turns ratio)

---

### Nuclear Physics (Y10 Unit)
Key concepts:
- **Atomic structure:** nucleus (protons + neutrons) surrounded by electrons
- **Isotopes:** same element, different number of neutrons → different mass number
- **Radioactive decay:** unstable nuclei emit radiation to become more stable
  - **Alpha (α):** helium nucleus (2p, 2n); stopped by paper; highly ionising; short range in air
  - **Beta (β):** fast electron emitted when neutron becomes proton; stopped by thin aluminium
  - **Gamma (γ):** electromagnetic radiation; stopped by thick lead or concrete; least ionising
- **Half-life:** time for half the radioactive atoms to decay
  - If initial count = 800 and half-life = 2 hours: after 6 hours (3 half-lives) → 800 → 400 → 200 → 100
- **Nuclear equations:** mass numbers and atomic numbers must balance on both sides
- **Uses of radiation:** medical imaging, cancer treatment, carbon dating, smoke detectors
