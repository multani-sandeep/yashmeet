## Session 1 — English · 45 min · PEA+ Structural Analysis

**Today's focus: Language Paper 1, Q3 — Structural analysis**

Q3 asks how the writer **structures** the whole text (or part of it) to interest the reader. Think about: how it begins · how ideas develop · shifts in focus · what is withheld or revealed · the ending.

Structural techniques to know: **non-linear narrative · focus shift · withholding information · zooming in / zooming out · contrast between opening and ending · use of time**

### Extract — Read carefully (5 min)

> *The letter had been sitting on the kitchen table for three days before she touched it. She had walked past it every morning — to the kettle, to the window, to the door — always finding some reason not to pick it up. It was a plain white envelope, her name written in handwriting she didn't recognise. That was the detail that stopped her. In all her forty years, she thought she knew every hand that might write to her. Apparently not.*
>
> *She sat down. She did not open it. She made a cup of tea. She drank it slowly, watching the envelope as though it might move. Then she slid her finger under the seal.*
>
> *The letter inside was dated six months earlier.*

### Task — Structural analysis (30 min)

> How does the writer structure this text to interest the reader? [8 marks]

Write **two** paragraphs focused on **structure**, not language.

**Paragraph 1 idea:** How the writer builds tension by withholding — we are given the letter but not the content; the character's avoidance mirrors the reader's curiosity.

**Paragraph 2 idea:** The shift in pace — the slow, repetitive delay (three days; walks past; the list of places) contrasted with the sudden decisive action at the end. Or: the twist of the date "six months earlier" withheld until the very end.

*Check against your Physics textbook (Boost Learning).*

### Part B — Calculations (20 min)

**Ohm's Law:** V = IR · **Power:** P = IV = I²R = V²/R · **Charge:** Q = It

1. A resistor has resistance 15 Ω and current 2 A flowing through it. Calculate the voltage across it.
2. A lamp has voltage 12 V and current 0.5 A. Calculate: (a) resistance (b) power
3. A circuit carries a current of 3 A for 4 minutes. Calculate the total charge transferred.
4. Two resistors of 6 Ω and 10 Ω are connected **in series** to a 16 V supply. Calculate: (a) total resistance (b) current in the circuit (c) voltage across each resistor.
5. The same two resistors are connected **in parallel** to a 12 V supply. Calculate: (a) total resistance (b) total current from the supply.

**Answers:** 30 V · 24 Ω, 6 W · 720 C · 16 Ω, 1 A, 6 V and 10 V · 3.75 Ω, 3.2 A

### Part C — Exam Question (15 min)

> (c) Calculate the power dissipated by the lamp. [2]
