## Y9 Topics — Consolidation

### Climate Change

**Causes:**
- **Natural:** Milankovitch cycles (changes in Earth's orbit); volcanic eruptions (short-term cooling from ash); sunspot activity
- **Human (enhanced greenhouse effect):**
  - Burning fossil fuels → CO₂ and methane emissions
  - Deforestation → less CO₂ absorbed + carbon released from trees
  - Agriculture → methane from livestock and rice paddies
  - Industry and transport → NOₓ and other greenhouse gases

**Greenhouse effect:** solar radiation passes through atmosphere; Earth radiates heat; greenhouse gases trap some heat → warms planet. Enhanced effect = more gases → more heat trapped.

**Impacts:**
- Rising sea levels (glacial melt + thermal expansion of oceans) → flooding of low-lying countries (Bangladesh, Maldives)
- More extreme weather: heatwaves, droughts, intense storms
- Habitat and biodiversity loss (coral bleaching, Arctic ice loss)
- Food insecurity: changing rainfall patterns affect crop yields
- Climate refugees: displacement of populations from uninhabitable areas

**Mitigation vs Adaptation:**
- **Mitigation:** reducing the cause — switching to renewables, carbon taxes, reforestation, electric vehicles
- **Adaptation:** managing the consequences — sea walls, drought-resistant crops, early warning systems

**International responses:**
- Paris Agreement (2015): countries commit to keeping warming below 2°C (ideally 1.5°C)
- COP conferences: annual global climate summits — limited enforcement, varying political commitment
- LEDC vs MEDC debate: who should bear the cost? Historical polluters (MEDCs) vs fast-growing emitters (LEDCs)

### Social Development

**Measuring development:**
- **GDP per capita:** total economic output ÷ population — measures wealth but not distribution or wellbeing
- **HDI (Human Development Index):** composite measure of life expectancy + education + income per capita
- **GII (Gender Inequality Index):** health, empowerment, and labour market participation by gender
- **Infant mortality rate:** deaths per 1,000 live births — sensitive indicator of healthcare and poverty

**Development continuum:** development is not binary (developed vs developing) but a spectrum. The term "Global South" is increasingly used instead of "LEDC/MEDC".

**Development gap:** the widening difference between richest and poorest countries.
- Causes: colonial legacy, debt, unfair trade terms, political instability, natural hazards, brain drain
- Example: Sub-Saharan Africa — low HDI despite abundant natural resources (resource curse)

**Top-down vs Bottom-up development:**
| | Top-down | Bottom-up |
|---|---|---|
| Control | Government or international organisation | Local community |
| Scale | Large | Small |
| Example | Large dam project, motorway | Micro-finance scheme, village well |
| Strength | Large impact, fast | Sustainable, locally owned |
| Weakness | May not meet local needs; displacement | Slow; limited reach |

### Superpowers and Geopolitics

**What makes a superpower?** Military power · economic strength · technological capacity · political influence · cultural soft power · natural resources · population size

**USA:** post-Cold War hyperpower; military dominance; dollar as global reserve currency; cultural reach (Hollywood, tech giants)

**China:** rising superpower; largest population, second-largest economy; Belt and Road Initiative (infrastructure investment across Asia/Africa)

**BRICS:** Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa — emerging economies challenging Western dominance

**Russia:** military superpower; vast energy reserves; declining economic influence but significant geopolitical leverage

**Prisoners of Geography (Tim Marshall):** physical geography shapes geopolitical decisions — e.g. Russia's need for warm-water ports; China's disputed South China Sea; Africa's colonial borders ignoring ethnic divisions

---

### Neo-Colonialism (Y9 Assessment Topic)

**Definition:** economic and political dominance of a more powerful country over a less developed one through indirect means (trade, investment, debt) — no formal colonial control but dependency persists.

**India-Africa example:**
- Africa supplies raw materials (cobalt, minerals); India profits from manufacturing
- Large-scale land deals (e.g. displacement in Ethiopia for Indian-leased farmland)
- **Counter-argument:** India funds infrastructure, shares technology, few political conditions
- **Conclusion:** mainly neo-colonial but with cooperative elements

**China in Africa:** Belt and Road Initiative → ports, roads, railways built; critics argue debt-trap diplomacy leads to dependency; supporters note infrastructure benefits

## Y10 Preview Topics

Y10 Geography typically extends into physical geography and further human geography units. Topics vary by exam board — these are common GCSE Geography themes to preview.

### Population and Urbanisation
- **Population growth:** birth rate − death rate = natural increase; demographic transition model (DTM — 5 stages)
- **Population pyramids:** wide base = youthful population (high birth rate, high death rate); narrow base = ageing population (low birth rate, low death rate)
- **Migration:** push factors (poverty, conflict, natural disaster) and pull factors (jobs, safety, education); economic migrants vs refugees
- **Urbanisation:** growth of cities — more common in LEDCs as rural populations move for work; megacities (10m+ population)
- **Urban issues:** squatter settlements (favelas, shanty towns) — lack of services; urban regeneration in MEDCs

### Ecosystems and Biomes
- **Tropical rainforest:** high biodiversity; nutrient cycle; threats (deforestation, farming, logging, mining); sustainable management
- **Hot desert:** low rainfall; adaptations of organisms; development opportunities (solar, oil) and challenges (remoteness, extreme heat)

### Natural Hazards
- **Plate tectonics:** tectonic plates (continental + oceanic); plate boundaries (constructive, destructive, conservative) → earthquakes and volcanoes
- **Earthquakes:** measured on Richter scale; impact varies by wealth (Haiti 2010 vs New Zealand 2011)
- **Tropical storms:** formation conditions (warm ocean, 26°C+); impacts; preparation and prediction

## Exam Technique — Geography Extended Answers

Geography GCSE marks longer answers on **accuracy, use of case studies, and structure**.

**For any "Assess/Evaluate" question:**
1. State a view (e.g. "Climate change poses the greatest threat because…")
2. Evidence: use a named case study or statistic
3. Counter-argument: "However, some argue that…" with evidence
4. Conclusion: reach a clear judgement — which factor is most significant and why?

**Case studies to know for Y9 topics:**
- Climate change: Maldives (sea level rise) · Sahel region (drought/desertification)
- Development: Ethiopia (land deals) · Bangladesh (HDI, flooding, garment industry)
- Superpowers: China's Belt and Road Initiative · USA's global military presence
