## Y10 Preview Topics

### Respiration
**Aerobic respiration** (in mitochondria, requires oxygen):
C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + ATP (energy)

**Anaerobic respiration** (no oxygen — produces much less ATP):
- In animals: glucose → lactic acid
- In yeast: glucose → ethanol + CO₂ (fermentation)

**Oxygen debt:** lactic acid builds up during anaerobic respiration in muscles → must be broken down after exercise using extra oxygen (hence heavy breathing after exercise)

**Comparison:**
| | Aerobic | Anaerobic (animals) |
|---|---|---|
| Oxygen needed? | Yes | No |
| ATP produced | Large amount | Small amount |
| Products | CO₂ + H₂O | Lactic acid |
| Where | Mitochondria | Cytoplasm |

---

### Excretion
**Excretion:** removal of metabolic waste products from the body (not to be confused with egestion = removing undigested food).

**Organs of excretion:**
- **Lungs:** excrete CO₂ and water vapour
- **Liver:** produces urea from excess amino acids (deamination); secretes bile
- **Kidneys:** excrete urea, excess water, and mineral ions in urine

**The kidney:**
- Filtration: blood is filtered under pressure → small molecules (water, glucose, urea, ions) pass into the nephron tubule
- Selective reabsorption: all glucose, most water, some ions reabsorbed back into blood
- Remaining liquid = urine (urea, excess water, excess ions)

**Dialysis:** an artificial kidney machine. Blood passes over a membrane; waste molecules diffuse out into dialysis fluid. Used when kidneys fail.

**Kidney transplant vs dialysis:** transplant = better quality of life, no repeated sessions; dialysis = no surgery, no rejection risk but time-consuming.

### Homeostasis
**Homeostasis:** maintaining a constant internal environment despite external changes.

**Temperature regulation (thermoregulation):**
- Controlled by hypothalamus
- Too hot: vasodilation (more blood to skin surface → more heat lost) + sweating (evaporation cools skin) + hairs lie flat
- Too cold: vasoconstriction (less blood to surface) + shivering (muscle contraction generates heat) + hairs stand up (trap air layer)

**Blood glucose regulation:**
- Detected by pancreas
- Too high: beta cells secrete **insulin** → liver and muscles convert glucose to glycogen (glycogenesis)
- Too low: alpha cells secrete **glucagon** → liver converts glycogen back to glucose (glycogenolysis)
- **Type 1 diabetes:** beta cells destroyed (autoimmune) → no insulin produced → treated with insulin injections
- **Type 2 diabetes:** cells no longer respond to insulin → often diet and exercise, sometimes medication

**Negative feedback:** if a level rises above the set point, a response is triggered to bring it back down (and vice versa). Maintains homeostasis.

### Nervous System and Hormones

**Nervous system:**
- **Neurones:** sensory (sense organ → CNS), relay (within CNS), motor (CNS → effector)
- **Reflex arc:** receptor → sensory neurone → relay neurone → motor neurone → effector (bypasses brain for speed)
- **Synapse:** gap between neurones; neurotransmitter chemicals carry the signal across

**Hormones** (compared to nervous system):

| Feature | Nervous | Hormonal |
|---|---|---|
| Speed | Fast (milliseconds) | Slow (seconds to hours) |
| Duration | Short-lived | Long-lasting |
| Transmission | Electrical then chemical at synapse | Chemical via blood |
| Target | Specific cells (effectors) | Target organs |

Key hormones to know: insulin, glucagon, adrenaline, oestrogen, progesterone, testosterone, FSH, LH, ADH (anti-diuretic hormone — controls water reabsorption in kidneys)

### Ecology
**Key terms:**
- **Population:** all organisms of one species in an area
- **Community:** all populations in an area
- **Ecosystem:** community + abiotic environment
- **Habitat:** the place where an organism lives
- **Niche:** the role of an organism in its ecosystem (what it eats, when active, where it lives)

**Feeding relationships:**
- Food chain: producer → primary consumer → secondary consumer → tertiary consumer
- Food web: many interconnected food chains
- Biomass pyramids: biomass decreases at each trophic level (energy is lost as heat, movement, reproduction)
- ~10% of energy passes from one level to the next

**Carbon cycle:** photosynthesis (CO₂ → organic carbon) · respiration (organic carbon → CO₂) · decomposition · combustion of fossil fuels

**Nitrogen cycle:** nitrogen fixation (N₂ → nitrates, by bacteria) · nitrification · denitrification · decomposition

**Human impact:** deforestation, overfishing, pollution, greenhouse effect, acid rain — know cause, effect, and reduction strategy for each.

### Genetics and Inheritance
**DNA:** double helix; made of nucleotides (sugar, phosphate, base); base pairs A-T and C-G

**Gene:** section of DNA that codes for a protein (one gene → one protein)

**Mitosis:** cell division for growth and repair → produces 2 identical diploid cells

**Meiosis:** cell division for sexual reproduction → produces 4 genetically different haploid gametes (sperm/egg)

**Genetics vocabulary:**
- Allele: a version of a gene
- Dominant allele: expressed in both homozygous (AA) and heterozygous (Aa) individuals
- Recessive allele: only expressed in homozygous recessive (aa) individuals
- Genotype: the alleles an organism has (e.g. Aa)
- Phenotype: the observable characteristic (e.g. brown eyes)

**Monohybrid cross:** use a Punnett square to predict offspring ratios
- Aa × Aa → 25% AA : 50% Aa : 25% aa → 3 dominant phenotype : 1 recessive

**Sex determination:** females XX, males XY; sex chromosomes determine biological sex

**Genetic disorders:** sickle cell anaemia (recessive), Huntington's disease (dominant)

---

### Evolution and Natural Selection
**Natural selection (Darwin):**
1. Organisms show variation within a species
2. More offspring are produced than can survive
3. Those with favourable variations are more likely to survive and reproduce (survival of the fittest)
4. Favourable alleles are passed to offspring → increase in frequency over generations
5. Over many generations → new species can form

**Evidence for evolution:** fossil record, comparative anatomy, molecular evidence (DNA similarities)

**Antibiotic resistance:** bacteria with random mutations for resistance survive → reproduce → resistance spreads (a live example of natural selection)
