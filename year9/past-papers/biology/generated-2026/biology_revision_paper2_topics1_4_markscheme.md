## Section A: Multiple Choice (32 marks — 1 mark each)

### Topic 1: Characteristics of Living Organisms

**Q1.** **B** — Movement (swims), Nutrition (captures and digests organisms), Reproduction (produces eggs/offspring). Excretion and respiration are not evidenced in the observations; sensitivity rather than movement is the trap for distractor A.

**Q2.** **B** — Breaking down glucose releases energy (respiration) and produces CO₂ as a metabolic waste product expelled from the organism (excretion).

**Q3.** **C** — Photosynthesis is the process by which a plant obtains its food (organic molecules) using light energy. This is the characteristic of **nutrition**.  
*(Note: D is a distractor — photosynthesis does produce oxygen but that alone makes it look like respiration to weaker students.)*

**Q4.** **D** — The shoot grows upward (away from gravity/towards light) and roots downward: this is a **directional growth response** = sensitivity, and the actual change in direction requires **growth**. Movement in plants is growth-based.

**Q5.** **C** — Homeostasis = regulation of internal conditions = **control**.

**Q6.** **B** — **Excretion** is a universal characteristic. Photosynthesis is only in plants/some protoctists; locomotion is not universal (e.g. plants do not move their whole body); sexual reproduction is not universal (asexual reproduction exists).

**Q7.** **A** — From the table, both organisms share: reproduction (both produce offspring), excretion (both release CO₂), and growth. Organism Q also shows sensitivity but Organism P does not — so sensitivity is not shared. Answer A correctly lists reproduction, excretion, and growth **only** as shared.

**Q8.** **B** — Viruses cannot independently carry out respiration, excretion, nutrition, growth, movement, sensitivity or control — they can only replicate inside a host cell. Having a protein coat (C) or nucleic acid (D) does not address whether they are living; being small (A) is irrelevant.

### Topic 2: Variety of Living Organisms

**Q9.** **A** — Fungi: cell wall of **chitin**, not photosynthetic, feed saprotrophically, store carbohydrate as glycogen. Row A matches all these features. Row B = plant; Row C = animal; Row D = bacterium.

**Q10.** **C** — The definition of a species requires the ability to produce **fertile offspring**. Zedonks cannot produce offspring → they are not a species. Donkeys and zebras cannot produce fertile offspring together → they are not the same species.

**Q11.** **C** — Single-celled, has chloroplasts, has a nucleus, no chitin cell wall → this is a plant-like **protoctist** (e.g. Chlorella, Chlamydomonas). Bacteria have no nucleus; fungi have chitin walls and no chloroplasts; animals have no chloroplasts.

**Q12.** **A** — Row A correctly matches:
- Description 1 (chitin, hyphae, not photosynthetic) → **Fungus**
- Description 2 (no nucleus, circular DNA) → **Bacterium**
- Description 3 (single-celled, nucleus, no chloroplasts, engulfs food) → **Protoctist** (animal-like)
- Description 4 (cellulose, chloroplasts, starch) → **Plant**

**Q13.** **B** — *Quercus robur* and *Quercus petraea* share the genus *Quercus*; *Betula pendula* and *Betula pubescens* share the genus *Betula*. That is **two** genera.

**Q14.** **A** — Mammals: fur/hair (1), produce milk (2), warm-blooded (3). Gills (4) are not a mammalian feature — mammals breathe with lungs.

**Q15.** **C** — *Plasmodium* is a single-celled **protoctist**. It is not a bacterium, fungus, or virus.

**Q16.** **A** — Spiders (8 legs, 2 body segments) = **arachnids**; centipedes (many legs, many segments) = **myriapods**; beetles (6 legs, 3 body segments) = **insects**.

**Q17.** **B** — Bacteria: **no nucleus**, cell wall of **murein** (not cellulose), **circular DNA** in cytoplasm. Row B is the only row consistent with all three bacterial features.

**Q18.** **B** — Mucor is multicellular with a mycelium (network of hyphae); yeast is unicellular. Both are fungi and have cell walls of **chitin**. Row A is wrong (cellulose = plant); Row C is wrong (fungi are not photosynthetic); Row D is wrong (both are eukaryotic).

### Topic 3: Levels of Organisation

**Q19.** **B** — A group of cells all carrying out the same function (secreting enzyme) = a **tissue**.

**Q20.** **C** — Correct sequence: **Organelles → Cells → Tissues → Organs → Organ systems → Organism**.

**Q21.** **C** — Both a root hair cell and a xylem vessel are within the **root** (an organ), and both are part of the same **organism** (the plant). They are different cell types so not the same cell; root hair cells and xylem are different tissues; the question is about what they share in common = organ and organism.

**Q22.** **B** — An organ is made of **different types of tissues** working together to perform a function. The heart contains cardiac muscle tissue, nervous tissue, connective tissue, and epithelium — not just one cell type.

**Q23.** **C** — Mitochondria and ribosomes are sub-cellular structures = **organelles**.

**Q24.** **C** — Circulatory system → **Heart** ✓. Nervous system → brain/spinal cord/nerves (not kidney); Digestive system → stomach/intestine (not lung); Respiratory system → lungs/trachea (not liver).

### Topic 4: Cell Structure and Functions

**Q25.** **B** — Large permanent vacuole + cellulose cell wall + chloroplasts + nucleus = **plant cell**. Chloroplasts are the site of **photosynthesis**.

**Q26.** **B** — Actual length = image length ÷ magnification = 54 mm ÷ 600 = **0.09 mm**.

**Q27.** **C** — **Ribosomes** are the site of protein synthesis.

**Q28.** **D** — No nucleus, circular DNA free in cytoplasm, cell wall (not cellulose), no mitochondria, ribosomes = **bacterium** (prokaryote). Plants and fungi are eukaryotes with a nucleus; animals have no cell wall.

**Q29.** **D** — A root hair cell is a plant cell that:
- Has a cell wall (cellulose) ✓
- Has a nucleus ✓
- Has **no** chloroplasts (root cells are below ground, no photosynthesis) ✗
- Has mitochondria ✓
Row D matches: + cell wall, + nucleus, − chloroplasts, + mitochondria.

**Q30.** **C** — Aerobic respiration occurs in **mitochondria**. Heart muscle cells respire aerobically at a very high rate → many **mitochondria** needed.

**Q31.** **B** — Antibiotic X causes cells to burst → targets the **cell wall** (weak/absent wall → osmotic lysis). Antibiotic Y stops protein synthesis → targets **ribosomes** (site of protein synthesis).

**Q32.** **C** — In a hypertonic (more concentrated) solution: water leaves the vacuole by **osmosis** → vacuole shrinks → cytoplasm pulls away from cell wall = **plasmolysis**.

### Question 34 *(4 marks — 1 mark per correct calculation)*

Formula: **Magnification = Image size ÷ Actual size**  
Therefore: **Actual size = Image size ÷ Magnification**  
And: **Image size = Actual size × Magnification**

**(a)** Actual length of onion epidermal cell

Actual length = Image length ÷ Magnification  
= 48 mm ÷ 400  
= **0.12 mm** *(1 mark)*

**(b)** Image length of cheek cell

Image length = Actual length × Magnification  
= 0.06 mm × 200  
= **12 mm** *(1 mark)*

**(c)** Actual length of bacterial cell

Actual length = Image length ÷ Magnification  
= 15 mm ÷ 3000  
= **0.005 mm** *(1 mark)*  
*(Accept: 5 µm or 5 × 10⁻³ mm)*

**(d)** Magnification used to view pollen grain

Magnification = Image length ÷ Actual length  
= 35 mm ÷ 0.07 mm  
= **×500** *(1 mark)*
