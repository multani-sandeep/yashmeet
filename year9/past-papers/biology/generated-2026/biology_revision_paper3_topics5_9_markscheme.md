### Question 33 *(4 marks)*

**(a)** *(2 marks)*

- **Optimum temperature: 37 °C** ✓ *(1 mark — accept 37)*
  - Justification: The shortest digestion time (4 min) occurs at 37 °C, indicating the highest rate of reaction / maximum frequency of enzyme-substrate collisions per unit time. ✓

- **70 °C result (1 mark):**
  - At 70 °C no digestion occurs; the enzyme (amylase) has been denatured — the high temperature has permanently changed the shape of the active site so the substrate (starch) can no longer bind / enzyme-substrate complexes cannot form. ✓

*Accept: "bonds holding the active site shape are broken", "active site is no longer complementary to starch"*

*Do not accept: "enzyme is killed" without further explanation of active site/denaturation.*

---

**(b)** *(2 marks)*

- The time taken for starch digestion will **increase** / the rate of digestion will decrease ✓
- The competitive inhibitor has a shape complementary to the active site of amylase ✓; it occupies the active site, competing with starch (the substrate) for binding / reducing the frequency of productive enzyme-substrate collisions / fewer starch molecules can bind per unit time, so digestion is slower.

*Accept: "inhibitor blocks the active site so fewer starch molecules can bind at once"*

*Do not accept: "inhibitor denatures the enzyme" — a competitive inhibitor is reversible and does not permanently alter the active site.*

### Question 34 *(4 marks)*

**(a)** *(3 marks — 1 mark each)*

- **Layer A: Palisade mesophyll (layer)**
  - Function: site of most photosynthesis / contains the most chloroplasts / absorbs light energy for photosynthesis ✓

- **Layer B: Spongy mesophyll (layer)**
  - Function: allows gas exchange / CO₂ and O₂ diffuse between cells and air spaces / provides a large internal surface area for gas exchange ✓

- **Structure C: Stoma (stomatal pore) / stoma(ta)**
  - Function: allows CO₂ to enter the leaf for photosynthesis and O₂ / water vapour to exit / controls gas exchange and rate of transpiration ✓

*Accept alternative functions: e.g. "Layer B: stores starch / supports the leaf" for partial credit if clearly linked to a genuine function.*

**(b)** *(1 mark)*

In the absence of light, the plant cannot carry out photosynthesis ✓; without photosynthesis, glucose is not produced and cannot be converted to starch for storage, so no starch accumulates in the leaves — the iodine solution remains yellow-brown (negative result).

*Accept: "No light → no photosynthesis → no glucose made → no starch produced/stored"*

*Do not accept: "the plant dies in the dark" without reference to photosynthesis/starch.*
