### Question 11 — Heating Curve and States of Matter (10 marks)

**(a)** **69 °C** **[1]**

**(b)** 
- Region **P** = any section on the **upper plateau or above** where all substance is liquid (the flat section at 80°C or rising line above the upper plateau) **[1]**
- Region **Q** = the **plateau at 69°C** (where melting occurs — mixture of solid and liquid) **[1]**

**(c)** Any two points: **[2]**
- The heat energy supplied is used to break (overcome) the **intermolecular forces** / bonds between particles, not to increase the kinetic energy of particles
- Since temperature depends on average kinetic energy, and kinetic energy is not increasing, temperature remains constant
- All energy input goes into the potential energy of separation, not kinetic energy

**(d)**

| | Solid | Liquid |
|---|---|---|
| Arrangement | Regular, close-packed, fixed positions in a lattice **[1]** | Random/irregular arrangement, close together but not fixed **[1]** |
| Motion | Vibrate about fixed positions **[1]** | Move/flow freely past each other, random motion **[1]** |

**[4 marks total]**

**(e)** Stearic acid (*M*r = 284) diffuses **slower** than ethanol (*M*r = 46) because it has a larger *M*r / heavier particles. At the same temperature, heavier particles have the same average kinetic energy but lower speed. **[1]**

### Question 12 — Diffusion and Brownian Motion (8 marks)

**(a)(i)** The brown/orange colour of bromine gas slowly spreads/diffuses through the tube from the release point towards the other end. **[1]**

*(Accept: the gas spreads; the colour moves along the tube)*

**(a)(ii)** In the evacuated tube, bromine spreads **faster** / more quickly. **[1]**

In the original experiment, bromine molecules collide with air molecules, slowing their progress. In the evacuated tube there are no air molecules to obstruct the bromine, so it spreads more rapidly. **[1]**

**(b)(i)** The ring forms at the **same position** (still closer to the HCl end). **[1]**

**(b)(ii)** At higher temperature, both gases diffuse faster, but the **ratio of their speeds** depends only on their *M*r values (which are unchanged). NH₃ is still lighter than HCl, so it still travels proportionally further. The relative meeting point does not change. **[2]**

*(Award 1 mark for stating the ratio of speeds is unchanged; 1 mark for linking to unchanged Mr values)*

**(c)(i)** **Brownian motion** **[1]**

**(c)(ii)** Water molecules are in constant random motion and collide with the pollen grain from all sides. Because the collisions are random and unequal, the net force on the pollen grain is not zero and changes direction constantly, producing jerky random movement. **[1]**
