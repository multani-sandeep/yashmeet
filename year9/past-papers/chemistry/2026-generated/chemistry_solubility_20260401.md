**1.** A student dissolves 95 g of KNO₃ in 100 g of water at 60 °C. Using Table 1, which statement **best** describes this solution?

- **D** 170 g

- **C** Gas particles gain kinetic energy and can overcome intermolecular attractions to escape from the solution
- **D** Evaporation of the solvent reduces the amount of dissolved gas

**7.** The solubility curves of salts P and Q intersect at 45 °C. Below 45 °C, Q is more soluble than P. Which statement is **correct**?

- **A** At all temperatures, P and Q have the same solubility

- **C** Above 45 °C, P is more soluble than Q
- **D** Above 45 °C, Q is more soluble than P

---

**8.** A saturated solution of NH₄Cl at 50 °C is prepared using 200 g of water. The temperature is raised to 80 °C at constant volume. Using Table 1, which statement is **correct**?

- **A** The solution remains saturated because no more salt is present

- **C** Some NH₄Cl crystallises out because the solution becomes supersaturated
- **D** The mass of dissolved NH₄Cl decreases as temperature increases

- **A** 20 g
