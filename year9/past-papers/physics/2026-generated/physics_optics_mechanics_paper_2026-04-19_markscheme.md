### Question 3 — Dispersion and the Electromagnetic Spectrum (15 marks)

**(a)(i)** **Dispersion** (of white light). **[1]**

---

**(a)(ii)** Award **1 mark each** for three of:

- White light is a mixture of all colours of the visible spectrum, each with a different wavelength/frequency. **[1]**
- Different colours travel at slightly **different speeds** in glass (but all at $c$ in vacuum). **[1]**
- The refractive index of glass is **different for each colour** — glass is more optically dense for violet light than red light (higher $n$ for shorter wavelengths). **[1]**
- Because $n = c/v$, colours with higher $n$ are slowed more and bent (refracted) more by the prism. **[1]**

**[3]**

---

**(a)(iii)**

- Most refracted: **violet** (shortest wavelength, highest $n$, bent most) **[1]**
- Least refracted: **red** (longest wavelength, lowest $n$, bent least) **[1]**

**[2]**

---

**(b)(i)** Any **three** of:

- They are all **transverse** waves. **[1]**
- They all travel at the **same speed** in a vacuum: $c = 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m s}^{-1}$. **[1]**
- They can all travel through a **vacuum** (they need no medium). **[1]**
- They all carry **energy**. **[1]**
- They all undergo **reflection, refraction, diffraction and interference**. **[1]** *(max 3)*

**[3]**

---

**(b)(ii)**

$$\lambda = 700 \text{ nm} = 700 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m} = 7.0 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$$

$$f = \frac{c}{\lambda} = \frac{3.0 \times 10^{8}}{7.0 \times 10^{-7}} = 4.3 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$$

- Correct conversion of nm to m **[1]**
- Correct answer $f = 4.3 \times 10^{14}$ Hz (accept $4.28$–$4.29 \times 10^{14}$ Hz) **[1]**

**[2]**

---

**(b)(iii)**

- Harmful effect: **causes skin cancer / damages DNA** (accept: causes cataracts to eyes; premature skin ageing). **[1]**
- Beneficial use: **sterilisation / kills bacteria** (accept: treatment of vitamin D deficiency; fluorescent lighting; security marking / forged bank note detection; treatment of skin conditions such as psoriasis). **[1]**

**[2]**

---

**(c)** A balanced evaluative answer:

- The student is **partly correct**: radio waves and gamma rays differ greatly in **wavelength** (and therefore frequency and energy) — radio waves have wavelengths $> 0.1$ m; gamma rays have wavelengths $< 10^{-12}$ m. **[1]**
- However, they are **both electromagnetic waves** — they are both transverse, travel at $c$ in a vacuum, and require no medium; they are not "completely different types." **[1]**

### Question 5 — Newton's Laws, Momentum and Collisions (14 marks)

**(a)(i)** In a **closed system** (no external forces), the **total momentum before** a collision equals the **total momentum after** — momentum is conserved. **[1]**

*(Must include: closed/isolated system or no external net force, AND total momentum is unchanged.)*

**[1]**

---

**(a)(ii)**

$$p_\text{before} = (1200)(4.0) + (800)(0) = 4800 \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$$

$$p_\text{after} = (1200 + 800)\,v = 2000v$$

$$v = \frac{4800}{2000} = 2.4 \text{ m s}^{-1}$$

- Correct total momentum before $= 4800$ kg m s$^{-1}$ **[1]**
- Correct total mass after $= 2000$ kg **[1]**
- Correct answer $v = 2.4 \text{ m s}^{-1}$ **[1]**

**[3]**

---

**(a)(iii)**

$$KE_\text{before} = \tfrac{1}{2}(1200)(4.0)^2 = \tfrac{1}{2}(1200)(16) = 9600 \text{ J}$$

$$KE_\text{after} = \tfrac{1}{2}(2000)(2.4)^2 = \tfrac{1}{2}(2000)(5.76) = 5760 \text{ J}$$

$$KE_\text{lost} = 9600 - 5760 = 3840 \text{ J}$$

- Correct $KE_\text{before} = 9600$ J **[1]**
- Correct $KE_\text{after} = 5760$ J **[1]**
- Correct $KE_\text{lost} = 3840$ J **[1]**
- States **inelastic** because kinetic energy is **not conserved** (KE is lost) **[1]**

**[4]**

$$950\,v_\text{rocket} = 30\,000$$

$$v_\text{rocket} = \frac{30\,000}{950} = 31.6 \text{ m s}^{-1}$$
