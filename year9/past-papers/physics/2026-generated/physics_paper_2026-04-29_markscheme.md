## Question 3 — Newton's Laws and Forces (15 marks)

**(a)** An object will remain at **rest**, or continue to move at **constant velocity in a straight line**, unless acted upon by a **resultant (net) force**. **[2]**

*(Award 1 mark for each underlined concept correctly included: at rest / constant velocity AND resultant/unbalanced force. Award 2 only if both ideas are present.)*

**[2]**

---

**(b)(i)**

$$a = \frac{F}{m} = \frac{24}{8} = 3 \text{ m s}^{-2}$$

- Correct substitution **[1]**
- Correct answer $a = 3 \text{ m s}^{-2}$ **[1]**

**[2]**

---

**(b)(ii)**

$$a = \frac{40}{8} = 5 \text{ m s}^{-2}$$

- Correct answer $a = 5 \text{ m s}^{-2}$ **[1]**

**[1]**

---

**(b)(iii)**

Resultant force $= 40 - 8 = 32$ N

$$a = \frac{32}{8} = 4 \text{ m s}^{-2}$$

- Correct resultant force $= 32$ N **[1]**
- Correct use of $F = ma$ **[1]**
- Correct answer $a = 4 \text{ m s}^{-2}$ **[1]**

**[3]**

---

**(c)(i)**

$$W = mg = 70 \times 10 = 700 \text{ N}$$

- Correct answer $W = 700$ N **[2]**

*(Award 1 mark for correct substitution if arithmetic error.)*

**[2]**

---

**(c)(ii)**

- As air resistance increases, the **resultant (net) downward force decreases** (weight is constant, upward air resistance increases). **[1]**
- Therefore, the acceleration **decreases** (becomes smaller), because $a = F_\text{net}/m$ and $F_\text{net}$ is getting smaller. **[1]**

**[2]**

---

**(c)(iii)**

- **Terminal velocity** is the constant (maximum) speed reached when air resistance equals weight (upward force = downward force). **[1]**
- At terminal velocity, the **resultant force is zero**, so there is no acceleration — the skydiver falls at constant speed. **[1]**

**[2]**

---

**(d)** Every action has an equal and opposite reaction / for every force exerted by object A on object B, object B exerts an equal and opposite force on object A. **[1]** (Any clearly stated correct example, e.g. a book resting on a table pushes down on the table, the table pushes up on the book with an equal force.) *(No separate mark for example — included in the [1].)*

*(Note: question says [1] — the law and example together earn the single mark. Accept a wide variety of examples: walking, swimming, rocket propulsion, etc.)*

**[1]**

## Question 4 — Momentum (12 marks)

**(a)** Momentum is the product of an object's **mass** and its **velocity** ($p = mv$). **[1]**

Unit: **kg m s$^{-1}$** **[1]**

**[2]**

---

**(b)(i)**

$$p = mv = 0.5 \times 12 = 6 \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$$

- Correct answer $p = 6 \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$ **[2]**

*(Award 1 mark for correct substitution if arithmetic error.)*

**[2]**

---

**(b)(ii)** Taking the original direction as positive:

$$p_\text{initial} = +6 \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$$

$$p_\text{final} = 0.5 \times (-8) = -4 \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$$

$$\Delta p = p_\text{final} - p_\text{initial} = -4 - 6 = -10 \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$$

- Correct assignment of sign to final velocity **[1]**
- Correct answer $\Delta p = -10 \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$ (magnitude $10$; accept $|\Delta p| = 10 \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$ if direction is clearly stated as "towards the wall" or "opposite to original direction") **[1]**

**[2]**

---

**(c)(i)** In a **closed system** (no external net force), the **total momentum before** a collision equals the **total momentum after** — total momentum is conserved. **[1]**

*(Must include: closed/isolated system AND total momentum unchanged.)*

**[1]**

---

**(c)(ii)**

$$p_\text{before} = (2)(3) + (1)(0) = 6 \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$$

$$p_\text{after} = (2)(1) + (1)v_B = 2 + v_B$$

Setting equal:

$$2 + v_B = 6 \implies v_B = 4 \text{ m s}^{-1}$$

- Correct total momentum before $= 6$ kg m s$^{-1}$ **[1]**
- Correct momentum equation after the collision **[1]**
- Correct answer $v_B = 4 \text{ m s}^{-1}$ **[1]**

**[3]**

---

**(c)(iii)** Change in momentum of trolley B:

$$\Delta p_B = m_B \times v_B - 0 = 1 \times 4 = 4 \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$$

$$F = \frac{\Delta p}{\Delta t} = \frac{4}{0.04} = 100 \text{ N}$$

- Correct $\Delta p_B = 4$ kg m s$^{-1}$ (ECF from $v_B$) **[1]**
- Correct answer $F = 100$ N **[1]**

**[2]**

## Question 5 — Work Done, Energy and Power (15 marks)

**(a)** Work done is the product of a force and the distance moved **in the direction of that force** ($W = Fd$). **[1]**

Work is only done when the object **moves** (there must be displacement in the direction of the force). **[1]**

**[2]**

---

**(b)(i)**

$$W = Fd = 35 \times 60 = 2100 \text{ J}$$

- Correct answer $W = 2100$ J **[2]**

*(Award 1 mark for correct substitution if arithmetic error.)*

**[2]**

---

**(b)(ii)**

$$P = \frac{W}{t} = \frac{2100}{40} = 52.5 \text{ W}$$

- Correct use of $P = W/t$ **[1]**
- Correct answer $P = 52.5$ W (ECF) **[1]**

**[2]**

---

**(c)(i)**

$$E_p = mgh = 0.4 \times 10 \times 5 = 20 \text{ J}$$

- Correct substitution **[1]**
- Correct answer $E_p = 20$ J **[1]**

**[2]**

---

**(c)(ii)**

$E_k = 20$ J **[1]**

Explanation: by the **principle of conservation of energy**, if there is no air resistance, all the GPE is converted to KE as the ball falls, so $E_k = E_p = 20$ J. **[1]**

**[2]**

---

**(c)(iii)**

$$E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \implies 20 = \frac{1}{2}(0.4)v^2$$

$$v^2 = \frac{20 \times 2}{0.4} = 100$$

$$v = 10 \text{ m s}^{-1}$$

- Correct rearrangement of $E_k = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$ **[1]**
- Correct substitution **[1]**
- Correct answer $v = 10 \text{ m s}^{-1}$ (ECF) **[1]**

**[3]**

---

**(d)**

- **Principle of conservation of energy**: energy cannot be created or destroyed; it can only be **transferred** from one form to another. The total energy in a closed system remains constant. **[1]**
- The "lost" energy is transferred to the **surroundings as thermal energy (heat)** due to the friction/drag of air resistance — the air and the ball are both slightly warmed. **[1]**

**[2]**

## Question 6 — Pressure (8 marks)

**(a)**

Equation: $P = \dfrac{F}{A}$ **[1]**

Unit of pressure: **Pascal (Pa)** (accept N m$^{-2}$) **[1]**

**[2]**

---

**(b)(i)**

$$W = mg = 15 \times 10 = 150 \text{ N}$$

- Correct answer $W = 150$ N **[1]**

**[1]**

---

**(b)(ii)**

$$P = \frac{F}{A} = \frac{150}{0.05} = 3000 \text{ Pa}$$

- Correct substitution **[1]**
- Correct answer $P = 3000$ Pa **[1]**

**[2]**

---

**(b)(iii)** Pressure **increases**. **[1]**

Because the same force (weight) now acts over a **smaller area**, and $P = F/A$ — smaller area means greater pressure. **[1]**

**[2]**

---

**(c)**

$$P = \rho g h = 1000 \times 10 \times 4 = 40\,000 \text{ Pa}$$

- Correct answer $P = 40\,000$ Pa ($40$ kPa) **[1]**
