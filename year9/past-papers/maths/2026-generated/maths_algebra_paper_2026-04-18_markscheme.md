# SECTION A: Algebraic Fractions (18 marks)

## Question 1

**(a)** $\frac{4x^2y^3}{10xy^5}$

**Working:** Cancel common factors: $\frac{4}{10} \cdot \frac{x^2}{x} \cdot \frac{y^3}{y^5} = \frac{2x}{5y^2}$

**Answer:** $\dfrac{2x}{5y^2}$ **[B1]**

---

**(b)** $\frac{x^2 - 16}{x^2 + 2x - 8}$

**Working:**
- Factorise numerator: $(x+4)(x-4)$ **[M1]**
- Factorise denominator: $(x+4)(x-2)$ **[M1]**
- Cancel $(x+4)$: $\dfrac{x-4}{x-2}$ **[A1]**

**Answer:** $\dfrac{x-4}{x-2}$

---

**(c)** $\frac{3x^2 + x - 2}{x^2 - 1}$

**Working:**
- Factorise numerator: $(3x - 2)(x + 1)$ **[M1]**
- Factorise denominator: $(x+1)(x-1)$ **[M1]**
- Cancel $(x+1)$: $\dfrac{3x-2}{x-1}$ **[A1]**

**Answer:** $\dfrac{3x-2}{x-1}$

---

## Question 2

**(a)** $\frac{3}{x+2} - \frac{1}{x-3}$

**Working:**
- Common denominator: $(x+2)(x-3)$ **[M1]**
- $= \dfrac{3(x-3) - 1(x+2)}{(x+2)(x-3)}$ **[M1]**
- $= \dfrac{3x - 9 - x - 2}{(x+2)(x-3)} = \dfrac{2x-11}{(x+2)(x-3)}$ **[A1]**

**Answer:** $\dfrac{2x-11}{(x+2)(x-3)}$

---

**(b)** $\frac{x+1}{x-2} + \frac{x-3}{x+1}$

**Working:**
- Common denominator: $(x-2)(x+1)$ **[M1]**
- Numerator: $(x+1)^2 + (x-3)(x-2)$ **[M1]**
- $(x^2 + 2x + 1) + (x^2 - 5x + 6) = 2x^2 - 3x + 7$ **[M1]**
- **Answer:** $\dfrac{2x^2 - 3x + 7}{(x-2)(x+1)}$ **[A1]**

Note: Denominator may also be left as $x^2 - x - 2$ — accept either form.

---

## Question 3

**(a)** $\frac{4}{x-1} = \frac{3}{x+2}$

**Working:**
- Cross multiply: $4(x+2) = 3(x-1)$ **[M1]**
- $4x + 8 = 3x - 3$ **[M1]**
- $x = -11$ **[A1]**

**Answer:** x = -11

---

**(b)** $\frac{x+3}{x-1} - \frac{x-2}{x+4} = 1$

**Working:**
- Common denominator: $(x-1)(x+4)$ **[M1]**
- $(x+3)(x+4) - (x-2)(x-1) = (x-1)(x+4)$ **[M1]**
- Expand LHS: $(x^2 + 7x + 12) - (x^2 - 3x + 2) = x^2 + 3x - 4$ **[M1]**
- $10x + 10 = x^2 + 3x - 4$
- $x^2 - 7x - 14 = 0$ ... wait — re-check:
  - LHS: $x^2 + 7x + 12 - x^2 + 3x - 2 = 10x + 10$
  - RHS: $x^2 + 3x - 4$
  - $10x + 10 = x^2 + 3x - 4$
  - $0 = x^2 - 7x - 14$
- Use quadratic formula: $x = \dfrac{7 \pm \sqrt{49 + 56}}{2} = \dfrac{7 \pm \sqrt{105}}{2}$ **[A1]**

**Answer:** $x = \dfrac{7 + \sqrt{105}}{2}$ or $x = \dfrac{7 - \sqrt{105}}{2}$ (exact form required)

_Accept decimal approximations x ≈ 8.62 or x ≈ -1.62 if working shown clearly._

# SECTION B: Solving Equations — Linear and Quadratic (14 marks)

## Question 4

**(a)** $\frac{3(2x-1)}{4} - \frac{x+5}{6} = 2$

**Working:**
- Multiply through by 12 (LCM of 4 and 6): $3 \times 3(2x-1) - 2(x+5) = 24$ **[M1]**
- $9(2x-1) - 2(x+5) = 24$
- $18x - 9 - 2x - 10 = 24$ **[M1]**
- $16x - 19 = 24$
- $16x = 43$
- $x = \dfrac{43}{16} = 2\dfrac{11}{16}$ **[A1]**

**Answer:** $x = \dfrac{43}{16}$

---

**(b)** $5 - \frac{2}{3}(4x + 9) = \frac{x-1}{2}$

**Working:**
- Multiply through by 6: $30 - 4(4x + 9) = 3(x-1)$ **[M1]**
- $30 - 16x - 36 = 3x - 3$ **[M1]**
- $-6 - 16x = 3x - 3$
- $-3 = 19x$
- $x = -\dfrac{3}{19}$ **[A1]**

**Answer:** $x = -\dfrac{3}{19}$

---

## Question 5

**(a)** $x^2 - 5x - 14 = 0$

**Working:** Factorise: $(x - 7)(x + 2) = 0$ **[M1]**

**Answer:** x = 7 or x = -2 **[A1]**

---

**(b)** $2x^2 + 7x - 15 = 0$

**Working:** Factorise: $(2x - 3)(x + 5) = 0$ **[M1]**

**Answer:** $x = \dfrac{3}{2}$ or $x = -5$ **[A1]**

---

**(c)** $3x^2 - 4x = 7$

**Working:**
- Rearrange: $3x^2 - 4x - 7 = 0$ **[M1]**
- Factorise: $(3x - 7)(x + 1) = 0$
- **Answer:** $x = \dfrac{7}{3}$ or $x = -1$ **[A1]**

---

**(d)** $(2x - 3)^2 = 25$

**Working:**
- Square root both sides: $2x - 3 = \pm 5$ **[M1]**
- $2x - 3 = 5 \Rightarrow x = 4$; or $2x - 3 = -5 \Rightarrow x = -1$ **[A1]**

**Answer:** x = 4 or x = -1

# SECTION C: Factorising (14 marks)

## Question 6

**(a)** $12x^3y^2 - 8x^2y^3 + 20x^4y$

**Working:** HCF is $4x^2y$

**Answer:** $4x^2y(3xy - 2y^2 + 5x^2)$ **[B2]**
_(B1 for correct HCF identified, B1 for correct bracket)_

**(b)** $9x^2 - 49y^2$

**Working:** Difference of two squares: $(3x)^2 - (7y)^2$

**Answer:** $(3x + 7y)(3x - 7y)$ **[B2]**
_(B1 for recognising DOTS structure, B1 for correct factorisation)_

---

**(c)** $4a^4 - 25b^2$

**Working:** Difference of two squares: $(2a^2)^2 - (5b)^2$

**Answer:** $(2a^2 + 5b)(2a^2 - 5b)$ **[B2]**
_(B1 for recognising DOTS structure, B1 for correct factorisation)_

---

**(d)** $x^2 - 3x - 40$

**Working:** Find two numbers that multiply to -40 and add to -3: $(-8)$ and $(+5)$

**Answer:** $(x - 8)(x + 5)$ **[B2]**
_(B1 for correct method, B1 for correct answer)_

**(e)** $6x^2 - 11x - 10$

**Working:** $ac = -60$; find factors: $-15$ and $+4$
$6x^2 - 15x + 4x - 10 = 3x(2x - 5) + 2(2x - 5) = (3x + 2)(2x - 5)$ **[M1 + A1]**

**Answer:** $(3x + 2)(2x - 5)$

**(f)** $2x^2 + 13x + 15$

$2x^2 + 3x + 10x + 15 = x(2x + 3) + 5(2x + 3) = (x + 5)(2x + 3)$ **[M1 + A1]**

**Answer:** $(x + 5)(2x + 3)$

---

**(g)** $4x^2 - 12x + 9$

**Working:** Perfect square: $(2x - 3)^2$ — check: $4x^2 - 12x + 9$ ✓ **[M1 + A1]**

# SECTION D: Simultaneous Equations (12 marks)

## Question 7

**(a)** $3x - 2y = 13$ ... (1)
$\ \ \ 5x + 4y = 11$ ... (2)

**Working:**
- Multiply (1) by 2: $6x - 4y = 26$ ... (1') **[M1]**
- Add (1') and (2): $6x - 4y + 5x + 4y = 26 + 11$
- $11x = 37 \Rightarrow x = \dfrac{37}{11}$ **[A1]**
- Substitute into (1): $3 \times \dfrac{37}{11} - 2y = 13$
- $\dfrac{111}{11} - 2y = \dfrac{143}{11}$
- $2y = \dfrac{111 - 143}{11} = -\dfrac{32}{11} \Rightarrow y = -\dfrac{16}{11}$ **[M1, A1]**

**Answer:** $x = \dfrac{37}{11}$, $y = -\dfrac{16}{11}$

_Note to marker: Accept equivalent fractions or exact decimals. Award M1 for correct elimination strategy, A1 for each correct value._

---

**(b)** $\dfrac{x}{3} + \dfrac{y}{2} = 4$ ... (1)
$\ \ \ 2x - y = 6$ ... (2)

**Working:**
- Multiply (1) by 6: $2x + 3y = 24$ ... (1') **[M1]**
- From (2): $y = 2x - 6$ **[M1]**
- Substitute into (1'): $2x + 3(2x - 6) = 24$
- $2x + 6x - 18 = 24$
- $8x = 42 \Rightarrow x = \dfrac{42}{8} = \dfrac{21}{4}$ **[A1]**
- $y = 2 \times \dfrac{21}{4} - 6 = \dfrac{21}{2} - 6 = \dfrac{9}{2}$ **[A1]**

**Answer:** $x = \dfrac{21}{4}$, $y = \dfrac{9}{2}$

---

**(c)** $y = x + 2$ and $x^2 + y^2 = 52$

**Working:**
- Substitute $y = x + 2$ into $x^2 + y^2 = 52$: $x^2 + (x+2)^2 = 52$ **[M1]**
- $x^2 + x^2 + 4x + 4 = 52$
- $2x^2 + 4x - 48 = 0$
- $x^2 + 2x - 24 = 0$ **[M1]**
- Factorise: $(x + 6)(x - 4) = 0$ **[M1]**
- $x = 4 \Rightarrow y = 6$; or $x = -6 \Rightarrow y = -4$ **[A1, A1]**

**Answer:** $(x, y) = (4, 6)$ or $(x, y) = (-6, -4)$

_Award M1 for substitution; M1 for expanding/simplifying to correct quadratic; M1 for factorising; A1 for each solution pair (both values correct)._

# SECTION F: Rearranging Formulae (8 marks)

## Question 9

**(a)** $v^2 = u^2 + 2as$, make $u$ the subject.

**Working:**
- $u^2 = v^2 - 2as$ **[M1]**
- $u = \pm\sqrt{v^2 - 2as}$ **[A1]**

**Answer:** $u = \pm\sqrt{v^2 - 2as}$

_Accept $u = \sqrt{v^2 - 2as}$ (positive root only) for full marks in a physics context, but ideally $\pm$ is shown._

---

**(b)** $T = 2\pi\sqrt{\dfrac{L}{g}}$, make $L$ the subject.

**Working:**
- $\dfrac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\dfrac{L}{g}}$ **[M1]**
- Square both sides: $\dfrac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \dfrac{L}{g}$ **[M1]**
- $L = \dfrac{gT^2}{4\pi^2}$ **[A1]**

**Answer:** $L = \dfrac{gT^2}{4\pi^2}$

---

**(c)** $\dfrac{a-b}{a+b} = k$, make $a$ the subject.

**Working:**
- $a - b = k(a + b)$ **[M1]**
- $a - b = ka + kb$
- $a - ka = kb + b$ **[M1]**
- $a(1 - k) = b(k + 1)$
- $a = \dfrac{b(k+1)}{1-k}$ **[A1]**

**Answer:** $a = \dfrac{b(k+1)}{1-k}$   or equivalently   $a = \dfrac{b(1+k)}{1-k}$

# SECTION G: Sequences and nth Term (6 marks)

## Question 10

**(a)** Sequence: 5, 9, 13, 17, 21, ...

**Working:** Common difference = 4; first term = 5
$n$th term $= 4n + 1$ **[M1 + A1]**
_(Check: $n = 1$: $4(1) + 1 = 5$ ✓; $n = 2$: $4(2) + 1 = 9$ ✓)_

**Answer:** $4n + 1$

---

**(b)(i)** $n$th term: $n^2 - 3n + 5$. Find the 7th term.

**Working:** $7^2 - 3(7) + 5 = 49 - 21 + 5 = 33$

**Answer:** 33 **[B1]**

---

**(b)(ii)** Find $n$ such that $n^2 - 3n + 5 = 75$

**Working:**
- $n^2 - 3n + 5 = 75$
- $n^2 - 3n - 70 = 0$ **[M1]**
- $(n - 10)(n + 7) = 0$ **[M1]**
- $n = 10$ or $n = -7$ (reject $n = -7$ as $n$ must be positive) **[A1]**

**Answer:** The 10th term equals 75.

# SECTION H: Word Problems — Form and Solve (8 marks)

## Question 11

**(a)** Rectangle with length $(3x + 2)$ cm and width $(x - 1)$ cm; area = 54 cm².

**Working:**
- Area: $(3x + 2)(x - 1) = 54$ **[M1]**
- Expand: $3x^2 - 3x + 2x - 2 = 54$
- $3x^2 - x - 2 = 54$
- $3x^2 - x - 56 = 0$ **[A1]** _(shown as required)_
- The quadratic does not factorise over integers; apply the quadratic formula: **[M1]**
  $$x = \dfrac{1 \pm \sqrt{1 + 4 \times 3 \times 56}}{6} = \dfrac{1 \pm \sqrt{673}}{6}$$
- Reject negative root (width $x - 1$ must be positive, so $x > 1$):
  $x = \dfrac{1 + \sqrt{673}}{6} \approx 4.49$ **[A1]**

**Answer:** Width $= x - 1 \approx 3.49$ cm; Length $= 3x + 2 \approx 15.47$ cm.

_Accept exact form $\dfrac{1 + \sqrt{673}}{6}$ or correctly rounded decimal (3 s.f.). Award M1 for forming the equation, A1 for correct $3x^2 - x - 56 = 0$, M1 for quadratic formula, A1 for correct positive value and dimensions._

---

**(b)** Two numbers differ by 5; sum of squares = 193.

**Working:**
- Let the numbers be $x$ and $x + 5$ (or $x$ and $y$ with $x - y = 5$ and $x^2 + y^2 = 193$) **[M1]**
- $(x)^2 + (x+5)^2 = 193$
- $x^2 + x^2 + 10x + 25 = 193$ **[M1]**
- $2x^2 + 10x - 168 = 0$
- $x^2 + 5x - 84 = 0$
- $(x + 12)(x - 7) = 0$ **[M1]**
- $x = 7$ or $x = -12$ **[A1]**

**Answer:** The two numbers are **7 and 12**, or **-12 and -7**.

_(Both pairs are valid; award full marks for either or both.)_

# SECTION I: Algebraic Proof (10 marks)

## Question 12

**(a)** Sum of three consecutive even numbers is always a multiple of 6.

**Model Answer:**
Let the three consecutive even numbers be $2n$, $2n + 2$, and $2n + 4$, where $n$ is an integer. **[M1]**

Sum $= 2n + (2n + 2) + (2n + 4) = 6n + 6 = 6(n + 1)$ **[M1]**

Since $n$ is an integer, $(n + 1)$ is an integer, so the sum is a multiple of 6. **[A1]** (conclusion stated)

**[3 marks total]**
_Award M1 for correct algebraic representation; M1 for correctly simplifying; A1 for correct conclusion with reason._

---

**(b)** Prove that $(n + 3)^2 - (n - 1)^2 \equiv 8(n + 1)$ for all values of $n$.

**Model Answer:**

LHS $= (n+3)^2 - (n-1)^2$ **[M1]**

$= (n^2 + 6n + 9) - (n^2 - 2n + 1)$ **[M1]**

$= n^2 + 6n + 9 - n^2 + 2n - 1$

$= 8n + 8$

$= 8(n + 1)$ = RHS **[A1]**

_Alternatively, use difference of two squares:_
$= [(n+3) + (n-1)][(n+3) - (n-1)] = [2n + 2][4] = 8(n+1)$ **[full marks for this method too]**

**[3 marks total]**

---

**(c)** Product of two consecutive odd numbers is always one less than a multiple of 4.

**Model Answer:**
Let the two consecutive odd numbers be $2n - 1$ and $2n + 1$, where $n$ is an integer. **[M1]**

Product $= (2n - 1)(2n + 1)$ **[M1]**

$= 4n^2 - 1$ **[M1]**

$= 4n^2 - 1 = 4(n^2) - 1$

Since $n^2$ is an integer, $4n^2$ is a multiple of 4. Therefore $4n^2 - 1$ is one less than a multiple of 4. **[A1]** (conclusion stated)

**[4 marks total]**
_Award M1 for correct representation of consecutive odd numbers; M1 for multiplying; M1 for correct expanded form; A1 for conclusion with full justification._
