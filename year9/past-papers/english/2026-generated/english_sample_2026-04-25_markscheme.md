# English Practice Question — Mark Scheme — 2026-04-25

## Section B: Structured Questions

**Q1.**

**(a) [2 marks]**
Award 1 mark for each valid detail identified from the first paragraph, up to a maximum of 2 marks.
- The ground had a "huge hole" (in which the cylinder lay).
- The turf and gravel seemed "charred".
- It looked as if there had been "a sudden explosion".
- The impact caused a "flash of fire".

*Accept reasonable paraphrasing.*

**(b) [6 marks]**
- **Level 3 (5–6 marks):** Clear and detailed explanation of how language/details create unease and mystery. Uses well-selected quotations. Shows understanding of the writer's methods (e.g., sensory details, reactions of the crowd).
- **Level 2 (3–4 marks):** Some explanation of the language/details. Uses relevant quotations. Makes valid points but may lack depth in analyzing *how* the effects are created.
- **Level 1 (1–2 marks):** Simple comments on the text. Quotations may be missing or overly long.
- **0 marks:** Nothing worthy of credit.

*Indicative content (what points students might make):*
- **Sensory language:** The "continuous, loud hissing noise" creates a sense of danger, unpredictability, and suggests there is something active and alive inside.
- **Physical reactions/details:** The fact that people "could not approach within twenty yards of it" because it was "so hot" emphasizes its alien, dangerous nature and forces people to keep a cautious distance.
- **Crowd's reaction:** Onlookers "step back in alarm" and are "muttering to themselves", highlighting their unease, fear, and confusion about the unknown ("what might be inside"). The mystery is amplified by their speculation and quiet panic.

**(c) [4 marks]**
- **3–4 marks:** A thoughtful evaluation that considers both sides or presents a strong, well-supported argument for one side. References to the text are apt and integrated.
- **1–2 marks:** A simple response that offers a basic opinion with limited textual support.

*Indicative content:*
- **Agree:** In the first paragraph, the reaction seems quite casual. Henderson and Ogilvy have even "gone away to breakfast" because "nothing was to be done," which feels very mundane and everyday. People in the second paragraph are "mostly standing, but some sitting" as if casually watching a spectacle rather than an invasion.
- **Disagree:** The physical description is incredibly dramatic ("colossal bulk," "charred," "explosion," "flash of fire"), which contrasts sharply with an everyday event. By the second paragraph, people are reacting with clear "alarm" to the "hissing noise," showing that the event is starting to feel threatening, unknown, and out of the ordinary.
