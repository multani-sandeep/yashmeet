Theology and Philosophy
Year 9 Autumn Term – Common Assessment Task – 40 mins

Task: Read the quote below from the Joker in The Dark Knight movie. This is the prompt for your dialogue – do not copy out the Joker quote. You should start with a response from Batman.

See, their morals, their “code”... it’s a bad joke, dropped at the first sign of trouble. They’re only as good as the world allows them to be. I’ll show you. When the chips are down, these, uh, these “civilized people,” they’ll eat each other. See, I’m not a monster. I’m just ahead of the curve... The only sensible way to live in this world is without rules.

<img>The Joker</img>

Write a dialogue between Batman and the Joker evaluating the Joker’s claim above.

Things to consider:

The Joker is an ethical egoist, the Batman is generally considered to be Kantian in his moral thinking
Use and explain key technical concepts associated with each ethical theory e.g. Categorical Imperative
Identify the most significant strengths and weaknesses of each position.
Use examples of moral dilemmas to illustrate the arguments
Contrast Kantian deontology with any form of Utilitarianism to highlight strengths/weaknesses
Mark Scheme
Attainment Grade: Exceeding (E)

I confidently explain the key differences between ethical egoism and Kantian deontology (including both the Principle of Universalisation and the Formula of Humanity in my evaluation of the Categorical Imperative)
I consistently apply correct technical terminology throughout my dialogue
I make highly effective use of examples to help illustrate my points
I identify and evaluate the main strengths and weaknesses of both ethical egoism and Kantian deontology
Attainment Grade: Good (G)

I show a good understanding of some of the differences between ethical egoism and Kantian deontology (including bat least one of the Principle of Universalisation and the Formula of Humanity in my explanation of the Categorical Imperative)
I apply correct technical terminology in my dialogue
I use at least one effective example to support a point made
I refer to strengths and weaknesses of both ethical egoism and Kantian deontology
Attainment Grade: Working Towards (W)

I show some understanding of the differences between ethical egoism and Kantian deontology
I have used some technical terminology, mostly accurately, in my dialogue
I use an example to support a point made
I refer to strengths and weaknesses of either ethical egoism or Kantian deontology
Attainment Grade: Concern (C)

I briefly outline the distinction between ethical egoism and Kantian deontology
I rarely use technical terms in my dialogue
