Chemists count atoms by mass, the same way that storekeepers count nails by mass. Atoms are so much smaller than nails, so the unit of counting is much larger. It is 600 000 000 000 000 000 000 000 (6 x 1023) atoms/ions/molecules. 
This number is used because scientist have shown that 6x 1023 atoms of any element have a mass equal to the relative atomic mass Ar of the element in grams. 
6x 1023 molecules of any element or compound have a mass equal to the relative molecular mass Mr in grams for covalent compounds and have a mass equal to the relative formula mass Mr in grams for an ionic compound. 
6x 1023 ions of any element or compound ions have a mass equal to Ar or Mr in grams

The number 6 x 1023 is called the Avogadro Constant in honour of Italian scientist Amedeo Avogadro.  
The amount of substance that contains 6 x 1023 particles is known as one mole (mol). 
  
One mole of an element contains 6 x 1023 atoms with a mass equal to the relative atomic mass in grams. 
The carbon-12 is the standard atom against which the masses of other atoms are compared. 
The relative atomic mass Ar of an element is the weighted average mass of its atoms compared to 1/12th the mass of a carbon-12 atom. 
e.g    One mole of magnesium, Mg,  has a mass of 24g 
24g of magnesium contain 6 x 1023 Mg atoms 
12g of magnesium contain 3 x 1023 Mg atoms and is is 0.5 mol of  Mg atoms 
240g of magnesium contain 6 x 1024 Mg atoms and is is 10 mol of  Mg atoms
