## SAMPLE 2 — Flashcard Deck

> **Why this works for language revision:**
> Flashcards force *active recall* — you retrieve the answer from memory rather than recognising it. This is one of the most well-researched revision techniques. Use a finger to cover the answer, or cut these out. Review daily: put cards you got right in a "done" pile; keep reviewing the ones you got wrong. Can also be imported into Anki or Quizlet for digital spaced repetition.

---

Use a finger or piece of paper to cover the **Answer** line before reading it.

---

**Card 1**
> Q: What are the preterite endings for regular **-AR** verbs?
> A: -é, -aste, -ó, -amos, -asteis, -aron
> *(Example: habl**é**, habl**aste**, habl**ó**...)*

---

**Card 2**
> Q: What are the preterite endings for regular **-ER / -IR** verbs?
> A: -í, -iste, -ió, -imos, -isteis, -ieron
> *(Example: com**í**, com**iste**, com**ió**...)*

---

**Card 3**
> Q: Give the full preterite of **ir** (to go) and **ser** (to be).
> A: fui, fuiste, fue, fuimos, fuisteis, fueron
> *(Both verbs share identical preterite forms — context tells them apart)*

---

**Card 4**
> Q: Give the preterite stem + endings for **hacer** (to do/make).
> A: Stem: hic- (hiz- for él/ella) + -e, -iste, -o, -imos, -isteis, -ieron
> *(hice, hiciste, hizo, hicimos, hicisteis, hicieron)*

---

**Card 5**
> Q: Spanish → English: **los huevos / la tortilla / el arroz / la paella**
> A: eggs / omelette / rice / paella

---

**Card 6**
> Q: Spanish → English: **el tomate / el mercado / el supermercado / comprar**
> A: tomato / market / supermarket / to buy

---

**Card 7**
> Q: English → Spanish: "I am going to eat" (near future using **ir + a + infinitive**)
> A: Voy a comer

---

**Card 8**
> Q: How do you form the **simple future tense** in Spanish?
> A: Take the infinitive + endings: -é, -ás, -á, -emos, -éis, -án
> *(Example: hablar → hablaré, hablarás, hablará...)*

---

**Card 9**
> Q: Translate: "Yesterday she went to the market and bought rice."
> A: Ayer ella fue al mercado y compró arroz.

---

**Card 10**
> Q: What are **reflexive verbs**? Give two examples.
> A: Verbs where the subject acts on itself; use reflexive pronouns (me, te, se, nos, os, se)
> *(Examples: levantarse — to get up; llamarse — to be called)*

## SAMPLE 3 — Story-based Exercises (Narrative context)

> **Why this works for language revision:**
> Grammar rules feel abstract without meaning. A story gives every verb and word a purpose, which helps your brain form stronger memories. Gap-filling in context mirrors real reading tasks and GCSE exam formats. Rewriting sentences in different tenses shows you understand the grammar, not just memorised it. This approach builds reading *and* writing skills simultaneously.

---
